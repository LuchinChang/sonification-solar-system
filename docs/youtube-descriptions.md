# YouTube Video Descriptions

## Video 1: Tutorial Walkthrough

**Title:** Solar System Sonification – Tutorial Walkthrough

**Description:**
A step-by-step walkthrough of the app's 11-step guided tutorial, starting on the default Pentagram of Venus pattern (Venus–Earth, 8-year cycle, 5 petals) at Sample Rate 500 and CPM 10. The tutorial begins by pressing D to open the dock, then spawning a circle — which defaults to a bass drum (bd). You'll hear deep, evenly spaced kick drum pulses as the playhead rotates around the circle every 6 seconds, firing each time it crosses one of the five petal arms. From there the video covers picking a new instrument, syncing changes with Cmd+Enter (listen for the green-flash confirmation), opening the live code panel with I to see the generated Strudel rhythm code, and browsing other planetary patterns with P.

## Video 2: Earth–Jupiter (Jupiter's Crown) at SR 35

**Title:** Earth–Jupiter (Jupiter's Crown) at Sample Rate 35

**Description:**
The Earth–Jupiter pattern ("Jupiter's Crown") — an 11-petaled rosette spanning a 12-year orbital cycle — demonstrated with the Sample Rate dialed down to just 35. At this setting, only 35 link lines are drawn between the two planets' positions, compared to the default 500. The result is a skeletal, wide-spaced geometry with far fewer intersection points for shapes to trigger. Expect to hear sparse, isolated drum hits with long silences between them — each strike clearly marking one of the 11-fold radial arms. The low intersection density turns what would normally be a dense polyrhythm into a slow, minimal pulse that exposes the underlying orbital symmetry one beat at a time.

## Video 3: Earth–Moon (Lunar Hexagon)

**Title:** Earth–Moon (Lunar Hexagon) – Geocentric Sonification

**Description:**
The Lunar Hexagon pattern viewed from a geocentric perspective — Earth sits at the center while lines trace between the Moon and the Sun over a 17.9-year simulation. The Moon completes roughly 12.37 synodic months per year, so every two New Moons the alignment rotates about 60°, gradually building a six-fold hexagonal symmetry. With shapes placed on this pattern at the default Sample Rate 500 and CPM 10, expect to hear rhythms with a strong six-beat accent structure — the playhead fires in clusters as it passes through each of the six dense petal regions, creating a more harmonically regular pulse than patterns with odd-numbered symmetries like Jupiter's Crown.
